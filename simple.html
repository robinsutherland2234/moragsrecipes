👨‍🍳 Chef’s notes — eating well within the rules

Small habits that keep Morag’s food varied, vibrant and full of flavour. This isn’t “free-from” food — it’s just good food.

Build a flavour base without onion & garlic
  • Garlic-infused oil, chives and the green tops of leek and spring onion give the allium flavour with none of the FODMAPs.
  • Brown your butter, and colour meat and fish well — the sticky golden “fond” left in the pan is pure flavour; deglaze it with stock or a squeeze of lemon.
  • Roast vegetables (carrots, tomatoes, squash, peppers) until caramelised; concentrating their sweetness replaces the depth onions usually bring.
  • Drop a parmesan rind into soups, risottos and sauces as they simmer for instant savoury body.
Layer umami — the “moreish” factor
  • Parmesan, mature cheddar, capers, olives, tamari, GF fish sauce and a little anchovy all add deep savouriness — a spoonful transforms a dish.
  • Fry a spoon of tomato purée in oil for a minute before adding liquid, to deepen sauces and soups.
  • A dash of tamari or fish sauce simply reads as “savoury”, not fishy.
Finish with acidity — it wakes everything up
  • A squeeze of lemon or lime, or a splash of vinegar, right at the end lifts a flat dish instantly.
  • Use citrus zest for perfume without adding any liquid.
  • If a dish tastes like it’s “missing something”, reach for salt or acid before anything else.
Season in layers
  • Season little and often as you cook, tasting as you go — not just at the end.
  • Save flaky salt for the final flourish; it gives bursts of seasoning and a little crunch.
Let fat carry the flavour
  • Bloom spices in hot oil or butter for 30 seconds — their flavour is fat-soluble and blossoms.
  • Finish dishes with cold butter, a good olive oil or a swirl of cream for silkiness and richness.
  • Keep flavoured butters in the fridge (lemon-herb, dill, caper) to melt over fish, chicken or potatoes.
Spice freely — just no chilli heat
  • Warm spices are all welcome: cumin, coriander, turmeric, ginger, cinnamon, fennel, cardamom, star anise, saffron and sweet/smoked paprika.
  • Smoked paprika brings smokiness and black or white pepper a gentle warmth — all without chilli.
  • Toast whole spices in a dry pan until fragrant before grinding or adding.
Build texture & contrast
  • Add crunch: toasted flaked almonds, pine nuts, sesame, toasted GF crumbs or oats, or lacy parmesan crisps.
  • Pair creamy with crisp, soft with fresh, warm with cool — contrast is what makes a plate exciting.
  • Quick-pickle cucumber or carrot (vinegar + a pinch of sugar, 10 min) for instant tang and crunch.
Fresh herbs, added at the end
  • Stir soft herbs (dill, parsley, basil, mint, chives, coriander, tarragon) in raw, off the heat, so they stay vivid and fragrant.
  • Blitz herbs with oil into a quick herb oil or pesto to drizzle over almost anything.
Keep variety: “the same, but different”
  • One ingredient, many moods — salmon with dill & lemon (Nordic), ginger & tamari (Japanese), or under a parmesan crust (Italian).
  • Work through the Around the World recipes to travel without leaving the safe list.
  • Change just the herb, the citrus or the finishing oil and a familiar dish feels brand new.
Cooking for a lighter appetite
  • Think small plates: two or three little dishes tempt more than one big plate.
  • Batch-cook soups, bakes and stews, then freeze single portions — a freezer full of safe, ready meals.
  • Make every bite count: enrich gently with butter, cheese, cream or olive oil, and lead with colour and aroma to tempt the appetite.
  • Soups, smoothies and rice puddings are gentle, nourishing ways to eat well on a low-appetite day.
Worth keeping in the cupboard
  • Garlic-infused oil, onion-free stock, tamari, GF fish sauce, tinned tomatoes, coconut milk, capers, olives, good parmesan.
  • Lemons, limes, fresh ginger, a pot of soft herbs, maple syrup, rice, GF pasta and GF oats.

Vanilla Porridge with Blueberry-Lemon Compote⏱ 8 min · Serves 1

Properly creamy oats under a quick burst-blueberry compote — a world away from a plain bowl of porridge.

✓ Safe for Morag: gluten-free · onion, garlic & chilli-free · low-FODMAP · no pulses · no red meat

Serves 1Prep 3 minCook 8 min

Ingredients

  • 50 g GF rolled oats
  • 2.5 dl milk
  • Pinch of salt
  • ¼ tsp vanilla
  • 1 handful blueberries
  • Zest and a squeeze of lemon
  • Drizzle of maple syrup

Method

  1. Simmer the oats with milk, salt and vanilla, stirring often, for 5–6 min until creamy.
  2. Meanwhile warm the blueberries with the lemon juice and a little maple until they burst into a loose compote.
  3. Spoon the compote through the oats, top with lemon zest and a final drizzle of maple.
👨‍🍳 Chef’s technique: Cooking porridge with a generous milk ratio and stirring constantly works the oats' starch into a silky, almost risotto-like cream; loosen it off the heat with a splash of cold milk just before serving.
💡 Tip: Maple rather than honey keeps it low-FODMAP.
Brown-Butter Banana & Cinnamon Oats⏱ 8 min · Serves 1

Nutty brown butter and caramelised banana turn a humble bowl of oats into something genuinely special.

✓ Safe for Morag: gluten-free · onion, garlic & chilli-free · low-FODMAP · no pulses · no red meat

Serves 1Prep 3 minCook 8 min

Ingredients

  • 50 g GF rolled oats
  • 2.5 dl milk
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 firm banana, sliced
  • 20 g butter
  • Pinch of cinnamon

Method

  1. Cook the oats in milk with a pinch of salt until creamy.
  2. In a small pan, cook the butter until the milk solids turn golden and smell of toffee.
  3. Add the banana to the brown butter for a minute to caramelise, then spoon over the oats with cinnamon.
👨‍🍳 Chef’s technique: Beurre noisette goes from gold to burnt in seconds — pull it off the heat the moment it smells nutty, the residual pan heat will finish it.
💡 Tip: A firm, just-ripe banana holds its shape and is gentler on the tummy.

✓ Safe for Morag: gluten-free · onion, garlic & chilli-free · low-FODMAP · no pulses · no red meat

Ingredients

  • 50 g GF rolled oats
  • 1 dl milk
  • 1 dl plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • ¼ tsp vanilla
  • Strawberries or blueberries, to top
  • Maple, to taste

Method

Baked Berry Oatmeal Slice⏱ 25 min · Serves 6

A warm, cakey baked oatmeal you can cut into squares — breakfast sorted for the whole week.

✓ Safe for Morag: gluten-free · onion, garlic & chilli-free · low-FODMAP · no pulses · no red meat

Serves 6Prep 10 minCook 25 min

Ingredients

  • 150 g GF rolled oats
  • 1 egg
  • 2.5 dl milk
  • 1 mashed banana
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp vanilla
  • Handful blueberries
  • Butter, for the tin

Method

  1. Whisk the egg, milk, mashed banana and vanilla together.
  2. Fold in the oats, baking powder and most of the berries.
  3. Spread into a buttered tin, scatter the rest of the berries on top.
  4. Bake at 180°C for 22–25 min until set and golden; cut into squares.
👨‍🍳 Chef’s technique: Mashed banana does double duty here — it sweetens without sugar and its starch binds the bake, so you need no flour at all.
💡 Tip: Freezes brilliantly; reheat a square for a grab-and-go breakfast.

✓ Safe for Morag: gluten-free · onion, garlic & chilli-free · low-FODMAP · no pulses · no red meat

Serves 1Prep 5 minCook 12 min

Ingredients

  • 1 large potato, coarsely grated
  • Butter
  • 1 egg
  • Snipped chives
  • Salt

Method

  1. Squeeze the grated potato bone-dry in a tea towel, then season.

Ingredients

  • 100 g GF oats, blended to flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1.5 dl buttermilk (or milk + squeeze lemon)

  • Pinch of salt
  • Butter, to fry

Method

Spanish Tortilla with Herbs⏱ 30 min · Serves 4

The great Spanish potato tortilla — soft, just-set and meltingly rich, sliced into thick wedges.

✓ Safe for Morag: gluten-free · onion, garlic & chilli-free · low-FODMAP · no pulses · no red meat

Serves 4Prep 10 minCook 30 min

Ingredients

  • 4 potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 6 eggs
  • Plenty of olive oil
  • Spring onion greens and chives
  • Salt

Method

  1. Gently confit the potato slices in olive oil over low heat until tender but not coloured; drain, keeping the oil.
  2. Fold the warm potato through the beaten eggs with the herbs and salt; rest 10 min.
  3. Cook slowly in a little of the oil, then flip with a plate and finish — the centre should stay just-set and creamy.
👨‍🍳 Chef’s technique: Cooking the potatoes slowly in oil (a confit, not a fry) keeps them silky, and pulling the tortilla while the middle is still slightly soft gives the classic juicy 'baboso' centre.
💡 Tip: Just as good warm or at room temperature; keeps for days.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (about 1.3 kg), or 6 bone-in thighs

  • 3 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 whole cloves
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp gluten-free fish sauce, plus more to taste

  • 1 tsp sugar

  • 2 tbsp garlic-infused oil

  • Knob of butter
  • Handful of parmesan

  1. Steep the saffron in a ladle of the warm stock.
